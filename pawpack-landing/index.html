The Material

A rope with a
life already lived.

Alpine climbing rope is engineered to catch a falling climber — rated for dynamic human loads between 10 and 80 kN. When a rope reaches the end of its viable climbing life, it retires with years of trust built into every fiber. PawPack gives that rope somewhere to go next.

A dog leash asks a fraction of what the wall demanded. The safety rating isn't a claim. It's a floor.

Follow the Rope
The Journey

From the wall
to the trail.

Six steps. One rope. Nothing wasted.

Gym Partnership
01.

Sourcing

Partner gyms and guide services hand off retired rope that has completed its climbing life. We verify spec, origin, and retirement date.

Inspection
02.

Intake

Each rope is logged, photographed, and tagged with its provenance card. The gym, the spec, the date — all travel with the rope.

Separation
03.

Sheath & Core

Sheath is stripped from core. Core becomes the leash. Sheath is cut into collar strips. Offcuts are saved for treat bags.

Richmond, VA
04.

Cutting

Rope is cut to trail-ready lengths in our Richmond workshop. Ends are heat-finished to prevent fraying. No bulk filler, no imported parts.

Hand Assembly
05.

Assembly

Solid brass hardware attached by hand. Each unit is inspected, tagged with provenance, and paired with a QR code linking back to the rope's history.

Trail-Ready
06.

To Your Door

Shipped in a compostable kraft box with a handwritten note from the maker. The rope's next life begins when you clip it on.

Questions

Ask the knot.

Retired alpine climbing rope is rated for dynamic human falls — typically 10 to 80 kN depending on spec. A dog leash operates far below that design threshold. We only source rope that has completed its climbing life on its own terms. It has never been used in a failure event.
Directly from partner climbing gyms and guide services. Every leash ships with the gym of origin, rope model, and retirement date on the hang tag. Scan the QR code and you can see photos from the rope's climbing life.
Every inch of every rope becomes a product. The core yields the leash. The sheath becomes collar strips. Offcuts are sewn into treat bags. End fibers are used as workshop thread. Nothing goes to landfill — and nothing is imported to make up the difference.
